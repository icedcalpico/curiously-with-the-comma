curiously, (with the comma)

a social card game with just enough structure to force you out your comfort-zone, but enough freedom to make every game new!

hi! i'm yen, and i love getting to know people, but i'm not the most observant person, and i need explicit answers to commit things to memory. sometimes, it feels like i'm interrogating someone instead of getting to know them, which is what this game solves for me. in the broader scheme of things, most connection building games don't have enough structure to make it feel like a game, so conversations are forced and fall off quickly. to mitigate this, i added a few mechanics to encourage not only critical thinking and reflection, but to also have some form of competition, except nobody loses no matter what moves are made.

to my friends, i would always ask random questions and preemptively defend it with "i'm just curious," and i realized that curiosity drives me to connect with people. the questions i had were purely because i wanted to know, and i hoped others would be just as curious in their own niche. before i added the ask or skip mechanic, i felt like i couldn't ask questions that required more vulnerability, and i noticed others tended to skip those questions as well. i remember asking one of the friends i had been playing the questions through with to create their own questions too. all of these pieces culminated to "curiously," which i'm more than excited to present here. thank you for checking out my work, even if it's not the super advanced neuroscience stuff. without further ado, here is "curiously," 

## how to play and rules

this is meant to be played by 2 people. you could play it with as many people as you'd like, but why would you do more than 2?

how to play:
- each player takes turns drawing a card from the deck. after a card is drawn, the player can either:
- ask the question; the turn ends when all parties have satisfactorily* answered the question (more on that below)
- skip the question; the player who draws it may choose to skip the question. some questions are not skippable and that will be indicated on the card. to skip a question, the player must come up with their own question and ask that instead. 
- the player who did not draw answers first

rules:
- satisfactorily means to have answered in a thoughtful and thorough manner. this is decided by both parties. this game is meant for connection, not evaluation, so there are no right or wrong answers. if a question is not answered satisfactorily, the a player is allowed to ask the other player to elaborate on their answer. 
- "i don't know" is a perfectly acceptable answer (given there was some thought into it, you're playing this game to see how well you know yourself too)
- if a card says "if unanswered, put it back in the deck," it should be put in the middle of the deck for the question to be revisited later if it's too early to answer the question. these questions are meant to be asked later in the game and are not skippable. 
- at the end, each player is allowed to ask one question that was not answered if desired.
- also at the end, each player is to write one (quality) question to be used in future games. make sure to sign your name, write it in print, and date it on the back!
- these questions are not meant to be gone through in one sitting. i've been playing it with the same three people over two months and we're still not done.
- most of these questions have been picked up from just hearing things, but the ones that have my name specifically are ones i'd like to take credit for or just my favorite ones :)

want to add a question to the deck? open a github issue with:

your question
whether it's skippable or not
your name 
your signature
the date the question was written (handwritten)
